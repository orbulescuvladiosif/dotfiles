# Write Requirements

Requirements state what the system must do — not how it will be built, not why it was chosen.

## What belongs

- Concrete conditions — entities, responsibilities, constraints
- Structural requirements before behavioral ones

## What doesn't belong

- Phases or build order — that's a plan
- Principles or rationale — those live in the PVD
- Implementation approach or design choices
- Anything that starts as "we should probably..."

## Process

Group by entity or concern — each group small and self-contained. Draft structural requirements first, then behavioral. When source material doesn't exist yet, mark remaining items as directional — they frame intent, not final shape. Merge overlaps. Cut plan, rationale, and vague intent.

## Done when

Every item is concrete, nothing overlaps, nothing missing that would block implementation
